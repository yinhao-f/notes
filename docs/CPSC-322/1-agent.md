# What is AI

Learning goals:
- Explain what AI is 
- Describe what an intelligent agent is 

## What is artificial intelligence?

Intelligent agents that think and act rationally in their environments. Rationality is more cleanly defined than "think and act like human". 

## What is an intelligent agent?

An intelligent agent
- is in some environment
- can make observations
- can act
- has goals or preferences
- has prior knowledge or beliefs, and can update beliefs based on new experiences

Early AIs were explicitly programmed. They layed down important conceptual foundations but had little practical success. 

### Search
Instead of hard-coding the algorithm, let the computer recoginize a solution and experiment by itself. This brought early success. 

### Machine learning

Give the computer examples of a pattern and tell it to find a rule (e.g. finding correlations). 

### Deep learning with neural networks

Machine learning models that take raw inputs like images. They are structured similar to brains. This was an old idea but only worked with recent GPU breakthroughs. Image and facial recognition was possible with this. 

### Reinforcement learning

A sequence of actions are taken before reaching rewards or punishment. Also came out in the 80s but breakthrough happened with **Monte Carlo Tree Search**. 

### Self-supervision and GenAI

Turn huge, unlabeled datasets into puzzles. AI will be able to generate inputs like it saw during training. 

### LLMs

Take a huge corpus of text and a huge neural network. Spend a lot of money on self-supervised training. 

### Chatbots (ChatGPT, etc.)

Fine tune the LLMs and adjust to user feedback. 

### Weaknesses

- Hallucinations
- Reasoning
